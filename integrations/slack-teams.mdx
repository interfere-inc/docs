Interfere integrates directly with Slack and Microsoft Teams so your team stays informed about issues the moment they're detected — without having to keep the Interfere dashboard open all day. You can receive notifications for new issues, status changes, comments, and assignments, and you can reply to issues directly from your chat tool of choice. Replies sync back into Interfere automatically, keeping the full conversation in one place.

## What you'll receive

Once connected, Interfere posts notifications to your chosen channel or workspace for the following events:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="New issue detected" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Interfere posts a message the moment a new issue is detected, including its title, priority, surface, and number of impacted users.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Status updates" icon="arrows-rotate">
    Get notified when an issue moves between statuses — for example, from **Investigating** to **Active** or **In Recovery**.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Comments and replies" icon="message">
    Receive a notification when a teammate posts a comment or replies to an existing thread on an issue.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Assignments" icon="user-check">
    See when an issue is assigned or reassigned to a team member, so the right people always know what's theirs.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Connecting Slack

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations settings">
    In the Interfere dashboard, go to **Settings → Integrations** and select **Slack**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize the Slack app">
    Click **Connect Slack**. You'll be redirected to Slack's OAuth authorization screen. Select the Slack workspace you want to connect, then click **Allow**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a default channel">
    After authorization, select the channel where Interfere should post notifications by default. You can use an existing channel (for example, `#incidents` or `#engineering`) or create a dedicated `#interfere` channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your notification preferences">
    Choose which event types should trigger notifications and set any filters (see [Filtering notifications](#filtering-notifications) below). Click **Save** to activate the integration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting Microsoft Teams

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations settings">
    In the Interfere dashboard, go to **Settings → Integrations** and select **Microsoft Teams**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize the Teams app">
    Click **Connect Teams**. You'll be directed through Microsoft's authorization flow. Sign in with an account that has permission to add apps to your Teams organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a team and channel">
    Choose the Team and channel where Interfere should post notifications. For example, you might route to the **Engineering** team under the **#incidents** channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your notification preferences">
    Set your notification event types and any filters, then click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Commenting from Slack or Teams

You can comment on any Interfere issue directly from a Slack or Teams notification — no need to open the dashboard.

**In Slack**, click the **Reply** button on an Interfere notification message to open a thread. Type your comment in the thread and submit it. Your message will appear on the issue's Activity timeline in Interfere within seconds.

**In Teams**, use the **Reply** action on the notification card to add your comment. It syncs to Interfere in the same way.

### Using @mentions

You can @mention teammates in your Slack or Teams replies just as you would in Interfere itself. If the mentioned person is a member of your Interfere workspace, they'll receive an in-app notification in addition to the Slack or Teams message.

```
@luke this looks like it might be related to the auth refactor from yesterday — 
can you take a look at the token validation logic in auth/reset.ts?
```

<Tip>
  Comments posted from Slack or Teams appear in the issue timeline with the author's Interfere identity, so your teammates can always see the full conversation thread in one place regardless of where each reply originated.
</Tip>

## Filtering notifications

Not every event needs to go to every channel. Interfere gives you fine-grained control over what gets routed where.

You can filter notifications by:

- **Surface** — Only post notifications for issues affecting a specific surface, such as **Website Frontend**, **iOS App**, or **API**.
- **Priority** — Limit notifications to issues above a certain priority threshold (for example, only **High** and **Critical** issues).
- **Assignee** — Route notifications to a channel only when an issue is assigned to a specific person or team.

To configure filters, go to **Settings → Integrations → Slack** (or **Teams**) and click **Edit Notification Rules**. You can create multiple rules with different channel targets and filter combinations.

<Note>
  If you connect both Slack and Microsoft Teams, you can configure each independently with different channels, filters, and event types. For example, you might send all issues to a Slack channel for your engineering team while routing only high-priority issues to a Teams channel watched by your on-call rotation.
</Note>

## Benefits

Connecting Interfere to your chat tool means your team is always informed, even when they're not actively monitoring the Interfere dashboard. Issues surface where your team already spends their time, comments stay in sync across both platforms, and there's no need to switch contexts just to leave a quick note on a problem.
