Interfere gives you fine-grained control over what each member of your team can access and change. Every user is assigned a role — either a built-in role or one you define — and that role determines which actions they can take across the workspace and within individual projects. Whether you're onboarding a large engineering org or managing a small product team, roles and permissions ensure people have exactly the access they need and nothing more.

## Built-in Roles

Interfere ships with three predefined roles that cover the most common team structures. These roles cannot be deleted, but they can be supplemented with custom roles.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="crown">
    Full access to all workspace settings, projects, and data. Can manage team members, billing, integrations, API keys, and security configuration.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Member" icon="user">
    Can view and interact with issues across all projects they have access to. Can assign issues, update status, and leave comments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Viewer" icon="eye">
    Read-only access. Can view issues, evidence, and timelines but cannot change status, assign work, or modify any settings.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Custom Roles

If the built-in roles don't match your team's structure, you can create custom roles with exactly the permission set you need. Custom roles are defined at the workspace level and can be assigned to any team member.

### Create a Custom Role

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Roles settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Team → Roles**. You'll see your existing roles listed, including the built-in Admin, Member, and Viewer roles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new role">
    Click **New Role** and give it a name and optional description. For example: `On-Call Engineer`, `Support Viewer`, or `Integration Manager`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select permissions">
    Toggle on the individual permissions this role should have. Permissions are grouped by category — Issues, Settings, Integrations, and Billing — to make configuration easier.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the role">
    Click **Create Role**. The new role will immediately appear in the role selector when you invite or edit team members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  A good starting point for a support team role is Viewer permissions plus the ability to comment on issues. This lets support staff track and contribute context without being able to change issue status or trigger actions.
</Tip>

## Assigning Roles to Team Members

You can assign or change a member's role at any time from the team management page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Team settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Team → Members**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the team member">
    Use the search bar or scroll the member list to find the person whose role you want to change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change their role">
    Click the role badge next to their name to open the role selector. Choose the new role and confirm. The change takes effect immediately — the member's next page load will reflect the updated permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you use [SCIM provisioning](/security/sso-scim), roles can be assigned automatically based on group membership in your identity provider. Manual role assignments in the Interfere dashboard take precedence over SCIM-assigned roles.
</Note>

## Workspace vs. Project-Level Access

Interfere supports two scopes of access control: workspace-level and project-level.

### Workspace-Level Access

Workspace-level permissions apply across the entire Interfere organization. Roles like Admin are workspace-scoped — an Admin can access all projects and all workspace settings without needing explicit project access.

### Project-Level Access

Project-level access lets you restrict which projects a member can see and interact with. This is useful when you have multiple products or teams that should operate independently.

- A member with workspace **Member** role can be restricted to specific projects.
- A member with workspace **Viewer** role can similarly be scoped to the projects relevant to their work.
- Admins always have access to all projects regardless of project-level settings.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I restrict a member to specific projects?">
    Navigate to **Settings → Team → Members**, click the member's name to open their profile, and use the **Project Access** section to select which projects they can see. Members outside a project cannot view its issues, evidence, or settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can a member have different roles in different projects?">
    Yes. A team member can have the **Member** role at the workspace level but be granted **Admin**-level permissions within a specific project. Project-level overrides apply only within that project — they don't elevate the member's workspace permissions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens when a new project is created?">
    By default, new projects are accessible to all workspace Admins. Members and Viewers must be explicitly granted access to new projects, or you can configure a workspace default to grant all members access to new projects automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Permission Inheritance

Permissions are resolved from the most specific scope to the least specific:

1. **Project-level role override** — if the member has a project-specific role, it takes precedence.
2. **Workspace-level role** — the member's default workspace role applies across all projects they can access.
3. **Built-in role baseline** — if using a built-in role (Admin, Member, Viewer), the permissions table above applies.

Custom roles follow the same inheritance order. When a permission is not explicitly set in a custom role, the member defaults to no access for that permission.

## Offline-First Architecture

Interfere is built on an offline-first architecture, which means the dashboard remains functional even when your network connection is intermittent or temporarily unavailable. Issue data, timelines, and evidence loaded during a connected session remain accessible while offline. Any actions you take — status changes, comments, assignments — are queued locally and synced to the server automatically once connectivity is restored.

<Note>
  Permission checks are always enforced server-side. Offline-queued actions that conflict with a permission change made while you were offline will be rejected on sync, and you'll be notified of any actions that couldn't be applied.
</Note>
