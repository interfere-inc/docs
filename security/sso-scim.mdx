Interfere supports enterprise identity management through SAML and OIDC single sign-on (SSO) and SCIM-based user provisioning. Once configured, your team members authenticate with your company's existing identity provider — no separate Interfere passwords required — and user accounts are created, updated, and deactivated automatically whenever you make changes in your IdP directory.

## Single Sign-On (SSO)

SSO lets every member of your organization log in to Interfere using your company's identity provider (IdP), such as Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, or any other SAML 2.0 or OIDC-compatible provider. This means your team uses a single set of credentials across all their tools, and you control access centrally.

### Supported Protocols

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="SAML 2.0" icon="id-card">
    Industry-standard XML-based protocol. Supported by Okta, Azure AD, OneLogin, Ping Identity, and most enterprise IdPs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="OIDC" icon="key">
    OAuth 2.0-based protocol. Supported by Google Workspace, Auth0, Azure AD, and modern identity platforms.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Domain Whitelisting

Domain whitelisting restricts Interfere access to users whose email addresses belong to your verified domain. When enabled, anyone attempting to sign in with an email outside your approved domains will be denied — even if they have a valid IdP session.

<Note>
  Domain whitelisting is enforced at login time. Existing users with non-whitelisted emails will lose access after the restriction is applied. Review your team's email addresses before enabling this setting.
</Note>

### Configure SSO

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your SSO settings">
    In the Interfere dashboard, navigate to **Settings → Security → SSO**. You'll see your organization's current SSO status and the fields needed to connect your IdP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retrieve your IdP metadata">
    In your identity provider's admin console, create a new SAML or OIDC application for Interfere. Download or copy the metadata document (for SAML) or the discovery URL and client credentials (for OIDC).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your IdP metadata in Interfere">
    Paste your IdP metadata URL or upload the XML file into the corresponding field. For OIDC, enter the discovery URL, client ID, and client secret. Interfere will parse the configuration and populate the required fields automatically.

    ```text
    # SAML — paste your IdP metadata URL, e.g.:
    https://your-idp.example.com/app/metadata.xml

    # OIDC — enter your discovery URL, e.g.:
    https://your-idp.example.com/.well-known/openid-configuration
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the Interfere callback URL into your IdP">
    Interfere will display an ACS URL (SAML) or redirect URI (OIDC) that you need to register in your IdP application settings. Copy it and paste it into the appropriate field in your IdP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection">
    Click **Test Connection**. Interfere will initiate a login flow in a new tab. If the test succeeds, you'll see a confirmation message. If it fails, check that your metadata and callback URLs match exactly on both sides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable SSO">
    Once the test passes, toggle **SSO Enabled** to on. New login attempts will be redirected to your IdP. Optionally enable **Enforce SSO** to prevent password-based logins entirely for members of your domain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If you enable **Enforce SSO** before all team members have IdP accounts, those members will be locked out. Ensure everyone in your Interfere workspace has a corresponding account in your identity provider first.
</Warning>

## SCIM Provisioning

SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) automates the user lifecycle in Interfere based on your IdP directory. When you add a user in your IdP, they are automatically provisioned in Interfere. When you remove or deactivate them, their Interfere access is revoked immediately — no manual cleanup required.

### What SCIM Manages

| Action in IdP | Result in Interfere |
|---|---|
| Create user | New member account created and invited |
| Update user (name, email) | Profile updated automatically |
| Deactivate user | Access suspended; data and history preserved |
| Delete user | Access revoked |
| Assign group | Role applied based on group mapping |

### Configure SCIM

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your SCIM settings">
    In the Interfere dashboard, navigate to **Settings → Security → SCIM**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy your SCIM endpoint URL">
    Interfere displays a unique SCIM endpoint URL for your organization in the SCIM settings panel. Copy this — you'll need it when configuring your IdP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy your bearer token">
    Below the endpoint URL, click **Generate Token** to create a SCIM bearer token. Copy it immediately — it is only shown once. Store it securely in your IdP's credential store.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure SCIM in your IdP">
    In your IdP, open the Interfere application you created for SSO (or create a new one for SCIM). Enter the SCIM endpoint URL and bearer token in the provisioning settings. Enable the provisioning actions you want to sync: create users, update user attributes, and deactivate users.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map IdP groups to Interfere roles (optional)">
    If your IdP supports group-based provisioning, map your IdP groups to Interfere roles. For example, an `engineering` group in your IdP might map to the **Member** role in Interfere. See [Roles and Permissions](/security/roles-permissions) for available roles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trigger an initial sync">
    Initiate a full sync from your IdP to provision all existing users into Interfere. Review the sync log in your IdP console to confirm all users were created successfully.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  SCIM and SSO are independent features but work best together. SSO handles authentication; SCIM handles provisioning. Enabling both gives you complete lifecycle management — users are created when they join your IdP and locked out the moment they leave.
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use SCIM without SSO?">
    Yes. SCIM provisioning and SSO are configured independently. You can enable SCIM to automate user management while still allowing password-based logins, or vice versa.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to an existing user's data when they are deactivated via SCIM?">
    Deactivating a user through SCIM immediately suspends their access to Interfere. Their account data, issue assignments, and activity history are preserved and remain visible to other members of your workspace.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I rotate my SCIM bearer token?">
    Navigate to **Settings → Security → SCIM** and click **Regenerate Token**. The old token is invalidated immediately. Update the new token in your IdP provisioning settings to restore the SCIM sync.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Which identity providers are supported?">
    Interfere works with any IdP that supports SAML 2.0 or OIDC for SSO, and any IdP that supports SCIM 2.0 for provisioning. This includes Okta, Azure Active Directory, Google Workspace, OneLogin, Ping Identity, and Auth0.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
