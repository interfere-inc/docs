Fixing production problems is rarely a solo effort. Engineers need to coordinate on root cause analysis, product managers need to weigh in on severity, and customer-facing teams need to know when a fix is landing. Interfere keeps everyone in the loop through a set of collaboration features built directly into the issue workflow — no context-switching to a separate tool required.

## Commenting on issues

Every issue has a comment thread embedded in its **Activity** timeline. You can leave a comment from the issue detail view at any time, and that comment becomes part of the permanent record of how the issue was investigated and resolved.

Comments are supported across three surfaces:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Interfere" icon="browser">
    Post comments directly in the Issue Detail view. Rich text, code snippets, and @mentions are all supported.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Slack" icon="slack">
    Reply to Interfere's Slack notifications inline. Your reply is synced back to the issue timeline in Interfere automatically.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Microsoft Teams" icon="microsoft">
    Post updates from Teams channels that are connected to your Interfere workspace. Replies flow back to the issue in real time.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

No matter where a comment originates, it appears in the Activity timeline alongside the agent's findings and system events. The timeline shows who posted the comment, when, and on which surface.

## Threaded comments

Comments on the Activity timeline support threading. This means you can reply directly to a specific comment rather than adding a new top-level message, keeping related discussion grouped together.

To start a thread:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hover over a comment">
    Move your cursor over any comment in the Activity timeline to reveal the action controls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Reply">
    Select **Reply** to open an inline reply input directly beneath the comment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write and submit your reply">
    Type your reply — you can @mention teammates or paste a code snippet — then press **Enter** or click **Submit**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Threaded replies are collapsed by default in the timeline so the main flow stays readable. A reply count indicator shows how many replies exist under each comment, and you can expand a thread at any time.

<Note>
  Threads started in Interfere can receive replies from Slack or Teams, and those replies will appear nested correctly under the original comment.
</Note>

## Assigning issues to team members

Every issue has a single assignee who is the designated owner for driving it to resolution. Assignments are visible on the issue card in the Inbox and in the issue header.

To reassign an issue:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the Assignee field">
    In the issue header, click the current assignee's name or avatar. A dropdown lists all workspace members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the new assignee">
    Choose the team member you want to assign. They will be notified immediately based on their notification preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assignment is recorded">
    An entry like *Assigned to Luke Shiels · 3 min ago* is added to the Activity timeline automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also assign issues directly from the Inbox without opening the full detail view: hover over an issue card and use the assignee control in the card footer.

## @mentions in comments

Use @mentions inside any comment to notify a specific team member. Type `@` followed by their name to surface the autocomplete picker.

```
@jakub have you seen this before? It looks like it might be related
to the auth refactor from yesterday.
```

Mentioned team members receive a notification immediately, regardless of whether they are subscribed to the issue. The mention links to their profile so anyone reading the timeline knows who was called in.

## Automatic routing

When Interfere detects a new issue, it doesn't land in an unowned queue — it is automatically routed to the team member best positioned to investigate it. Routing is based on the rules your workspace admin configures, and can factor in:

- **Surface ownership** — which team or individual owns a given surface (e.g., the iOS App surface routes to the mobile team)
- **Code ownership** — when Interfere links an issue to a specific file or module, it can route to the file's designated owner
- **On-call schedule** — if your team uses a rotation, the current on-call engineer receives the assignment

<Tip>
  Automatic routing means that high-priority issues like `#120 Broken password reset link` are owned from the moment they're detected — Luke Shiels was assigned automatically because he owns the authentication surface, not because someone triaged and delegated it.
</Tip>

Routing rules are configured in **Settings → Routing**. You can define rules by surface, by directory pattern in your codebase, or by issue type.

## Multiplayer: working an issue simultaneously

Multiple team members can view and work on the same issue at the same time. Interfere shows live presence indicators — small avatars in the issue header — to show which teammates are currently viewing the issue.

When several people are in a detail view simultaneously:

- **Comments appear in real time** without a page refresh
- **Status and priority changes** are reflected immediately for all viewers
- **New agent findings** appear in the Activity timeline for everyone at once

This is especially useful during a high-severity incident. When `#120 Broken password reset link` is affecting thousands of users, your on-call engineer, a product manager, and a customer success lead can all be in the issue simultaneously — each seeing the same live timeline as the agent surfaces findings and the fix progresses.

## Notification settings

You control how and when Interfere notifies you about issues.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| **Issue assigned to me** | Notify when an issue is assigned or reassigned to you |
| **Issue I'm subscribed to updated** | Notify on any change to issues you've subscribed to |
| **@mention** | Notify when someone @mentions you in a comment |
| **New issue in my surface** | Notify when a new issue is detected on a surface you own |
| **Daily digest** | A summary of open issues and their current status, delivered once per day |

Notification preferences are configured per channel — you might want in-app and email notifications for assigned issues, but only Slack pings for @mentions. Update your preferences in **Settings → Notifications**.
